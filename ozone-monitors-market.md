They offer high sensitivity and precision, making them suitable for environmental monitoring. Electrochemical monitors, on the other hand, employ chemical reactions to detect ozone levels, providing a cost-effective and portable solution for personal or industrial use. Each type serves specific applications, contributing to air quality management and pollution control efforts.</p></p>
