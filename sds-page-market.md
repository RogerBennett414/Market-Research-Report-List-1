<p><p>SDS-PAGE, or sodium dodecyl sulfate polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis, is a widely used technique in biochemistry and molecular biology for separating proteins based on their molecular weight. It utilizes the anionic detergent SDS to denature proteins, imparting a negative charge that allows them to migrate through a polyacrylamide gel in an electric field. This method is crucial for analyzing protein purity, assessing molecular weight, and characterizing protein interactions.</p><p>The SDS-PAGE market is experiencing notable growth, driven by the increasing demand for protein analysis in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and academic research. As more laboratories adopt advanced diagnostic techniques, the need for reliable protein separation methods is expanding. The rising focus on proteomics and personalized medicine further fuels this demand.</p><p>Technological advancements, such as the introduction of mini-gel systems and automated electrophoresis equipment, are enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of SDS-PAGE. Additionally, the trend towards automation in laboratory processes is likely to streamline workflows and reduce manual errors.
